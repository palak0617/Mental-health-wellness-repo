What To Do in an Emergency

These simple grounding steps can help you stabilize during mental health distress. They are not a replacement for professional help.

Anxiety Attack

  • Take slow, deep breaths — inhale for 4 seconds, exhale for 6.
  • Place your hand on your chest and remind yourself: "I am safe."
  • Drink a glass of cold water to help your body reset.
  • Look around and name 5 things you can see (grounding technique).
  • Focus on your senses - touch something textured, listen to calming sounds.

Panic Attack

  • Sit down and relax your shoulders, uncross your legs and arms.
  • Breathe using the 4-7-8 method (inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8).
  • Hold something cold (a metal object or cold bottle).
  • Focus on one comforting phrase like "This will pass" or "I am okay."
  • Use progressive muscle relaxation, tensing and releasing each muscle group.

Depression Episode

  • Move to a well-lit room or step outside for fresh air and sunlight.
  • Drink water or eat something small to regulate your energy.
  • Reach out to someone you trust — text or call a friend or family member.
  • Try the "3-minute activity rule": do one small task like making your bed.
  • Practice self-compassion - speak to yourself as you would to a dear friend.
