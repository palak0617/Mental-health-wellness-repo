Emergency Self-Help

These grounding techniques can help stabilize you during mental health distress. Remember, they complement but don't replace professional support.

Anxiety Attack

  • Take slow, deep breaths—inhale for 4 seconds, hold, exhale for 6 seconds
  • Place your hand on your chest and repeat: "I am safe"
  • Drink cold water to help reset your nervous system
  • Use the 5-4-3-2-1 grounding technique: name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste
  • Focus on your senses—touch something textured nearby

Panic Attack

  • Find a comfortable seated position and uncross your limbs
  • Practice 4-7-8 breathing: inhale for 4, hold for 7, exhale for 8
  • Hold something cold like an ice cube or cold water bottle
  • Repeat a calming mantra: "This will pass, I am okay"
  • Try progressive muscle relaxation—tense and release each muscle group

Depression Episode

  • Move to a well-lit space or step outside for sunlight and fresh air
  • Stay hydrated and eat something small if you haven't recently
  • Reach out to a trusted friend or family member, even just to say hello
  • Try the 3-minute rule: do one small task like making your bed
  • Practice self-compassion—speak to yourself as you would to a dear friend
